Get Outside

Outdoor Adventures

Paddling

Kayak the Childs River & Waquoit Bay

Launch right from the community boat ramp and paddle the scenic Childs River into Waquoit Bay. Head toward Washburn Island — a stunning 334-acre wildlife reserve accessible only by boat.

📍 Childs River · 30–45 min paddle to Washburn Island

Paddling

Eel Pond & Seapit River

Launch from Eel Pond in East Falmouth — right beside Washburn Island. Paddle up the Seapit River into Waquoit Bay for wildlife spotting: osprey nests, herons, and more.

🚣 Kayak rentals: RideAway Adventures · (508) 247-0827

Cycling

Shining Sea Bikeway

A beloved paved trail stretching over 10 miles from Woods Hole through Falmouth. Mostly flat with stunning coastal views — ideal for families and leisurely rides.

🚲 Rentals: RideAway Adventures · Corner Cycle Falmouth

Nature Walk

Ashumet Holly & Wildlife Sanctuary

Right in East Falmouth off Route 151 — 45 acres of nature trails winding through a rare holly forest. Great for birdwatching and quiet morning walks.

📍 Ashumet Rd, East Falmouth · Free to explore

Hiking

The Knob at Quissett Harbor

A short wooded trail leading to a rocky peninsula overlooking Buzzards Bay — one of the finest sunset spots on the Cape. Dogs welcome on leash.

📍 Quissett Harbor Rd, Falmouth · ~0.5 mi trail

Water Sports

Swimming & SUP

Beyond the club pool and association beach, Surf Drive Beach has calm waters perfect for swimming. Rent paddleboards from RideAway Adventures for delivery to your door.

🏄 SUP delivery available across Cape Cod

Fishing

Deep-Sea & Freshwater Fishing

Charter boats out of Falmouth Harbor for deep-sea fishing. Freshwater licenses available at local tackle shops. Kids under 15 fish freshwater license-free!

🎣 Charters depart Falmouth Harbor

Day Trip

Washburn Island Camping

Paddle 30–45 minutes from Childs River to reach this 334-acre state-owned island. Camp on the water at one of Cape Cod's last undeveloped gems. Reserve in advance.

📞 Waquoit Bay Reserve · (508) 457-0495

Family Fun

Woods Hole Marine Aquarium

The nation's oldest marine aquarium — and free admission! Don't miss the seal feedings and touch tanks. A wonderful half-day family outing just a short drive away.

📍 Woods Hole · Free admission · 15 min drive

Fresh From the Sea

Where to Eat

Falmouth is a seafood lover's paradise. These are the local favorites — from harborfront institutions to hidden gems the locals swear by.

01

The Flying Bridge

A classic Falmouth institution right on Falmouth Harbor. Panoramic water views, lobster rolls, fisherman's platters, and grilled swordfish. Outdoor deck is a must in summer.

⭐ Top Pick Seafood Harbor Views
~10 min
02

The Quarterdeck

Over 40 years of surf 'n' turf excellence on Main Street. Built with wood salvaged from a 1600s boatyard — it genuinely feels like dining below deck. Local fishermen supply the catch daily.

Seafood Historic Setting
~10 min
03

Pier 37 Boathouse

Elegant harborfront dining on Scranton Ave. Known for pancetta-seared scallops, sesame-encrusted Ahi tuna, and poke bowls. Perfect for a special occasion dinner.

Seafood Harbor Views Special Occasion
~12 min
04

Shipwrecked

Right on the beach at Falmouth Heights — seafood, sushi, and American fare with an unbeatable casual vibe. No reservations needed. Great after a beach day.

Seafood & Sushi Beachfront
~12 min
05

The Clam Man / Falmouth Fish Market

The freshest seafood on the Cape — this is where local chefs shop. Pick up PEI mussels, stuffed scallops, swordfish steaks, or clams for a home cook night at the house.

Fish Market Take Home
~8 min
06

The Barking Claw

A beloved local spot for lobster, chowder, and fried clams. Casual, fun, and always fresh — the kind of Cape Cod seafood shack you'll be telling friends about.

Seafood Casual
~10 min
07

Water Street Kitchen (Woods Hole)

A hidden gem on the waterfront — all seafood is sustainably sourced, partnered with the Monterey Bay Aquarium's Seafood Watch. Watch the Martha's Vineyard ferry while you dine.

Sustainable Seafood Water Views
~15 min
